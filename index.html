NeuralBoneReg: A Novel Self-Supervised Method for Robust and Accurate Multi-Modal Bone Surface Registration

Luohong Wu1, Matthias Seibold2, Nicola A. Cavalcanti1,3, Yunke Ao1,2, Roman Flepp1,2, Aidana Massalimova1,2, Lilian Calvet1,2, Philipp Fürnstahl1,2
1Balgrist University Hospital· 2ETH Zurich· 3OR-X
arXiv Code

Abstract

Background: In computer-assisted orthopedic surgery (CAOS), patient-specific surgical plans, that are utilized to execute surgical procedures precisely and consistently, are mainly derived from preoperative medical imaging data. To provide effective surgical guidance, most CAOS systems require an accurate and often cross-modal registration between intraoperative and preoperative imaging data, where the inherent high symmetry of bone shapes introduces a large number of local minima during the registration process. Existing multimodal registration approaches rely either on manual or automatic but anatomy-specific registration methods. Therefore, robust and accurate methods for automatic, anatomy-agnostic multimodal registration of bone surfaces would have a high clinical impact.

Methods: We propose NeuralBoneReg, a surface-based self-supervised pipeline tailored to multimodal bone surface registration in CAOS. NeuralBoneReg comprises an implicit neural unsigned distance field (UDF) module and a novel MLP-based registration module. The implicit neural UDF representation module learns a UDF from the bone model derived from preoperative imaging data. The registration module generates a set of transformation hypotheses to register a point cloud derived from the intraoperative imaging data with the preoperative imaging data based on our proposed registration loss. In contrast to traditional CPU-based optimizers, the registration module is designed for high parallelism and the efficient use of GPU resources. For each specimen, NeuralBoneReg operates in a self-supervised manner, without requiring any training data from other specimens or ground truth transformations. NeuralBoneReg and baseline methods are extensively evaluated on two publicly available datasets obtained from cadaveric experiments: a CT--ultrasound dataset of the fibula and tibia (UltraBones100k) and a CT--RGBD dataset of spinal vertebrae (SpineDepth). Furthermore, we evaluate the proposed method on a novel CT--ultrasound dataset of cadaveric specimens containing femur and pelvis (UltraBones-Hip) that will be made publicly available.

Results: Quantitative and qualitative results demonstrate the effectiveness of NeuralBoneReg. Specifically, NeuralBoneReg surpasses existing automatic methods, achieving a mean relative rotation error (RRE) of 1.63° and a mean relative translation error (RTE) of 1.85 mm on the UltraBones100k dataset; a mean RRE of 1.81° and a mean RTE of 1.87 mm on the UltraBones-Hip dataset; and a mean RRE of 3.03° and a mean RTE of 2.36 mm on the SpineDepth dataset. These results highlight the generalizability of NeuralBoneReg across different bone shapes and imaging modalities.

Conclusions: The robust and accurate registration performance of NeuralBoneReg demonstrates its potential to advance downstream applications in CAOS.
